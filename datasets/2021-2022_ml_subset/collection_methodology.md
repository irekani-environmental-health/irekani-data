The Irekani Project aimed to:

1. **Document agricultural practices** and agrochemical use in lake basin communities
2. **Assess perceived environmental quality** changes over recent decades
3. **Record biodiversity changes** as observed by agricultural workers
4. **Understand health perceptions** related to agrochemical exposure
5. **Evaluate safety practices** in agrochemical handling and application
6. **Generate community-useful data** for environmental health risk management

## ðŸ‘¥ Citizen Science Approach

### Participatory Research Design

The Irekani Project employed **Community-Based Participatory Research (CBPR)** principles:

1. **Community Involvement**: Local stakeholders engaged from project design through dissemination
2. **Capacity Building**: Training young researchers as Citizen Scientists
3. **Mutual Learning**: Academic researchers and community members as co-investigators
4. **Action-Oriented**: Results shared with communities for environmental health decision-making
5. **Culturally Sensitive**: Respect for local knowledge and indigenous perspectives

### Citizen Scientists

**Total**: 26 young university students

**Recruitment**:
- Students from different universities in MichoacÃ¡n
- Primarily from rural communities or familiar with agricultural contexts
- Many bilingual (Spanish/PurÃ©pecha)
- Interest in environmental health and social research

**Training** (40 hours total):
1. **Research Ethics** (8 hours)
   - Informed consent procedures
   - Participant confidentiality
   - Cultural sensitivity
   - Data privacy principles

2. **Interview Techniques** (12 hours)
   - Structured interview protocols
   - Active listening
   - Non-leading questions
   - Handling sensitive topics (health, income)

3. **Technology Training** (12 hours)
   - Progressive Web App (PWA) use
   - Audio recording protocols
   - Photography documentation
   - GPS location capture (anonymization)

4. **Field Safety** (4 hours)
   - Rural safety protocols
   - Transportation logistics
   - Emergency contacts
   - Paired field work

5. **Agricultural Context** (4 hours)
   - Regional crops and farming systems
   - Common agrochemicals
   - Environmental concerns in lake basins
   - Project objectives and scientific background

**Supervision**:
- Organized in teams of 2-3 students
- Each team supervised by research team member
- Regular check-ins during field work
- Weekly debriefing sessions

## ðŸ“± Data Collection Instruments

### Progressive Web App (PWA)

**Technology**: Custom-built Progressive Web Application

**Features**:
- Offline-first design (works without internet)
- Structured questionnaire with skip logic
- Automatic data validation
- Audio recording integration
- Photo documentation
- GPS location capture (anonymized at municipal level)
- Data encryption during transmission
- Automatic backup to secure server

**Advantages**:
- Standardized data entry across all Citizen Scientists
- Reduced transcription errors
- Real-time data validation
- Multimedia documentation
- Works on any smartphone/tablet
- No app store approval needed

### Questionnaire Structure

**Total**: 181 variables across 10 categories

1. **Geographic Information** (2 questions)
   - Municipality
   - Community type (automatically determined)

2. **Environmental Quality Perceptions** (20 questions)
   - Changes in water, air, soil quality
   - Climate pattern changes (rain, temperature, drought)
   - Landscape and biodiversity observations

3. **Biodiversity Loss** (50 questions)
   - Checklist of species groups observed to decrease
   - Categories: birds, mammals, reptiles, fish, invertebrates, plants
   - Local common names and scientific equivalents

4. **Agricultural Practices** (19 questions)
   - Farm size and tenure
   - Crops grown
   - Irrigation systems
   - Labor practices
   - Economic information

5. **Sociodemographic Characteristics** (9 questions)
   - Age, gender, education
   - Household composition
   - Healthcare access
   - Indigenous language (yes/no only, not which language)

6. **Agrochemical Safety Practices** (11 questions)
   - Personal protective equipment (PPE) use
   - Training and knowledge
   - Storage and disposal practices

7. **Agrochemical Application** (4 questions)
   - Frequency, timing, methods
   - Preventive vs curative use

8. **Pest and Disease Control** (5 questions)
   - Target organisms (insects, weeds, fungi, rodents)

9. **Agrochemical Compounds** (73 questions)
   - Checklist of specific products used
   - Commercial names standardized to active ingredients
   - Herbicides, insecticides, fungicides, rodenticides, fertilizers

10. **Health and Social Services** (3 questions)
    - Perceived health problems from agrochemicals
    - Symptoms experienced
    - Healthcare access

**Question Types**:
- Multiple choice (single selection)
- Multiple choice (multiple selection allowed)
- Binary (yes/no)
- Ordinal scales (better/same/worse)
- Numeric (continuous)
- Short text (later coded into categories)

**Skip Logic**:
- "If no agrochemical use â†’ skip safety practices section"
- "If no health problems â†’ skip symptoms question"
- Reduces participant burden and improves data quality

## ðŸŽ¤ Interview Protocol

### Participant Recruitment

**Sampling Strategy**: Convenience and snowball sampling
- Agricultural workers approached in fields, markets, community centers
- Referrals from initial participants
- Community leaders helped identify diverse participants
- No monetary compensation (ethical considerations)

**Inclusion Criteria**:
- Age 18 years or older
- Engaged in agricultural work in study municipalities
- Willing to provide verbal informed consent

**Exclusion Criteria**: None beyond age requirement

### Informed Consent

**Verbal Consent** (not written):
- Explanation of project objectives
- Description of data collection process
- Assurance of anonymity and confidentiality
- Right to refuse or stop interview at any time
- Right to skip questions
- How results will be used and shared

**Documented** via:
- Audio recording of consent process (later deleted)
- Checkbox in PWA confirming consent obtained
- Citizen Scientist signature

**Language**: Spanish or PurÃ©pecha, depending on participant preference

### Interview Process

**Duration**: 45-90 minutes (average 60 minutes)

**Setting**:
- Usually at participant's home or farm
- Private, comfortable location
- Minimal distractions

**Procedure**:
1. Introduction and informed consent (10 min)
2. Sociodemographic questions (5 min)
3. Agricultural practices (15 min)
4. Agrochemical use and safety (20 min)
5. Environmental and biodiversity perceptions (15 min)
6. Health questions (10 min)
7. Thanks and explanation of next steps (5 min)

**Audio Recording**:
- Full interview recorded (with consent)
- Used for quality control and quote verification
- Stored securely, never shared publicly
- Deleted after data validation complete

**Photography**:
- Photos of farms, crops, stored agrochemicals (with permission)
- No photos of participants' faces
- Used for documentation and context

## âœ… Data Quality Assurance

### During Collection

1. **Real-Time Validation**:
   - PWA checks for out-of-range values
   - Required fields must be completed
   - Logical consistency checks (e.g., farm size vs labor force)

2. **Paired Interviews** (subset):
   - 10% of interviews conducted by 2 Citizen Scientists
   - Inter-rater reliability assessment
   - Training feedback

3. **Supervisor Review**:
   - Daily review of completed interviews
   - Immediate feedback to Citizen Scientists
   - Identification of training gaps

### Post-Collection

1. **Data Cleaning**:
   - Standardization of open-text responses
   - Product name â†’ active ingredient conversion
   - Municipality name spelling standardization

2. **Validation Interviews** (10% sample):
   - Research team contacted subset of participants
   - Verified key responses (agrochemical use, health problems)
   - High concordance (>95%)

3. **Community Verification**:
   - Preliminary results presented to community groups
   - Participants invited to review findings
   - Corrections incorporated

4. **Outlier Investigation**:
   - Extreme values reviewed (e.g., farm size >100 ha)
   - Audio recordings checked
   - Corrected or flagged as appropriate

## ðŸ”¢ Sample Characteristics

### Final Sample Size

**Original Dataset**: ~500 interviews collected during field work (2021-2022)
**Published Complete Dataset**: 288 participants (after data cleaning and quality control)
**ML Subset**: 127 participants (definitive health perception responses)

**Note**: The original field work collected nearly 500 interviews. After rigorous data validation, quality control, and removal of incomplete or inconsistent responses, the final published dataset includes 288 high-quality records suitable for scientific analysis.

### Response Rate

- **Interviews collected**: ~500
- **Published dataset**: 288 participants (after quality control)
- **Retention rate**: 58% (from raw data to published dataset)

**Reasons for non-participation**:
- No time / too busy (45%)
- Not interested (30%)
- Suspicious of research purposes (15%)
- Language barrier (5%)
- Other (5%)

### Representativeness

**Not a probability sample**, but efforts made to ensure diversity:
- Range of farm sizes (0.5 to 100+ hectares)
- Multiple crop types
- Different farming systems (rainfed, irrigated)
- Age range: 18-85 years
- Education levels: no formal education to postgraduate
- Both genders (though agricultural workers predominantly male)

**Limitations**:
- Convenience sampling may miss certain populations
- Self-selection bias (those with strong opinions more likely to participate)
- Language barrier may have excluded some indigenous speakers

## ðŸ”’ Ethical Considerations

### Institutional Review

**Ethics Approval**: Formal IRB approval not obtained

**Justification**: Universidad de La CiÃ©nega del Estado de MichoacÃ¡n de Ocampo currently lacks an established ethics committee for non-clinical research

**Ethical Framework**: Declaration of Helsinki principles followed

**Risk Assessment**: Minimal risk to participants
- No physical interventions
- No collection of biological samples
- Standard survey questions
- Full anonymization

### Data Protection

1. **Anonymization**:
   - No names, addresses, phone numbers collected
   - GPS coordinates aggregated to municipal level
   - Exact dates removed
   - Community names removed
   - Small cell sizes suppressed if needed for privacy

2. **Secure Storage**:
   - Encrypted during transmission
   - Access restricted to research team
   - Audio recordings deleted after validation
   - Raw data with any identifiers never shared

3. **Participant Rights**:
   - Right to refuse participation
   - Right to skip questions
   - Right to withdraw (during interview)
   - Right to request data deletion

### Community Engagement

**Results Sharing**:
- Summary findings presented to communities
- Workshop with agricultural organizations
- Policy brief for local government
- Academic publications acknowledge community contribution

**Reciprocity**:
- Information sessions on agrochemical safety
- Connections to agricultural extension services
- Citizen Scientists remain community resources

## ðŸ“Š Data Management

### Database Structure

**Format**: PostgreSQL relational database (during collection)
**Export**: CSV files (for sharing)
**Encoding**: UTF-8
**Missing data codes**:
- `NA`: Not applicable
- `desconocido`: Unknown/don't know
- Empty: Declined to answer

### Data Processing Pipeline

1. **Collection**: PWA â†’ encrypted transmission â†’ secure server
2. **Initial Cleaning**: Automated scripts for standardization
3. **Manual Review**: Research team checks flagged entries
4. **Validation**: Cross-check subset with audio recordings
5. **Anonymization**: Remove all identifiers, aggregate GPS
6. **Export**: Generate CSV files for each dataset version
7. **Documentation**: Data dictionary, codebook, README
8. **Archiving**: Upload to Zenodo with DOI assignment

### Reproducibility

**Version Control**: All processing scripts on GitHub
**Seed Values**: Set for any random processes
**Dependencies**: Requirements.txt for Python packages
**Documentation**: Step-by-step processing documented
